## 3. Purpose
The Company is formed to engage in engineering consulting, including but not limited to embedded systems development, AI product development, software engineering, and any other lawful business activities permitted under Wyoming law.

## 4. Management
The Company is Member-Managed. The Member has full authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company, including the power to:
- Enter into contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company
- Open and manage bank accounts
- Borrow money and incur debt
- Hire employees and independent contractors
- Execute any documents necessary to conduct business

## 5. Capital Contributions
The Member shall contribute cash, property, or services to the Company as determined by the Member. No additional capital contributions shall be required without the Member's consent.

## 6. Profits and Losses
All profits and losses of the Company shall be allocated to the Member. All distributions of Company funds shall be made to the Member at the Member's discretion.

## 7. Tax Status
The Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity separate from the Member for federal tax purposes. All income and expenses shall be reported on the Member's individual tax return (Schedule C).

## 8. Dissolution
The Company shall dissolve upon:
- The decision of the Member to dissolve
- The death or incapacity of the Member (unless the Member has designated a successor in writing)

Upon dissolution, all Company assets shall be distributed to the Member after payment of debts and obligations.

## 9. Indemnification
The Company shall indemnify the Member against any liabilities incurred on behalf of the Company, to the fullest extent permitted by Wyoming law.

## 10. Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Wyoming.
