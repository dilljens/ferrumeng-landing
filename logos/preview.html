N5

No circles. Pure connection lines bundled into a bus of neural signals. Four parallel paths per F arm, varying opacities suggest depth. At favicon size they merge solid.

N6

Neural Dendrite
16
zero floating circles biologically accurate tree-like branching pyramidal neuron

A single pyramidal neuron where dendrite branches naturally form the F. Apical dendrite = vertical stem. Basal tufts = the arms. Only the soma (cell body) is a circle — and it's essential biology, not decoration.

N7

Neural Weave
16
two interwoven threads continuous lines signal + structure zero circles

Two continuous neural threads weaving over-and-under like a braided cable. One thread = signal (bright copper), other = structure (dark). They cross at junction points (no circles). The F emerges from their interplay.

N8

Neural Grid
16
rectangular mesh fabric active routing = F compute fabric feel zero circles

A neural routing mesh where most paths are dim (inactive) and the F is lit (active). Like watching a network router's heatmap. Grid crossings use tiny X-marks instead of circles. Structured, ordered, architectural.

N9

Neural Pulse Line
16
single continuous line EEG-style trace varying thickness zero circles organic flow

One uninterrupted flowing line that traces the F like a real-time neural activation. Enters bottom, loops through the vertical, sweeps the arms, exits. Varying line thickness suggests signal strength. No circles, no nodes.

IC body with 14 pins, neural F-traces etched into the die. The outer package says hardware. The copper core says intelligence. Both things at once.

Stripped-down chip with only 6 pins. Inside, the F is drawn with organic curved paths — not rigid PCB traces but biological neural pathways. The "mind" half is more visible at small sizes.

Inspired by real chip die photography (like the Intel 4004). I/O bond pads, sweeping bond wires, concentric functional rings, and repeating gate-array cells. The F is built from dense logic gates at the core.

Three stacked dies like HBM memory: neural accelerator (top) + cache (middle) + logic (bottom). The F is formed by through-silicon vias punching through all layers. A 3D chip architecture in 2D.

Quick picks:

Most favicon-friendly (Neural): Neural Minimal or Neural Traceline — no circles, all bold lines
Best storytelling (Neural): Neural Deep — ML engineers recognize the layer architecture; or Neural Dendrite — actual neuron morphology
Most visually striking (Neural): Neural Pulse — glowing activation heatmap; or Neural Hex Path — firing wave through tissue
Best no-circles pick: Neural Traceline (parallel bundles) or Neural Weave (two interwoven threads) or Neural Grid (mesh routing)
Best circles-only-on-F: F Trace (dotted letterform) or Connected Dots (junction nodes) or Constellation (10 star points)
Best hex-path: Hex Solid (reads as F at any size) or Hex Border (hollow, elegant) or Hex Swarm (density gradient)
Best overlapping solid hex: Hex Layers (3D forged metal) or Hex Cascade (waterfall flow) or Hex Stack (shingle texture)
Best completely new direction: Truss (structural engineering) or Aperture (precision optics) or Knot (mathematical elegance)
Best storytelling: Trilateration (intersection of three domains, right in the name) or Radar (detection + signals)
Best architectural feel: Isometric (CAD model) or Truss (bridge/tower structure) or Origami (folded planes)
Most favicon-friendly (Chip): Chip Simple — 6 pins, organic F inside, reads well small
Best storytelling (Chip): Chip Die Shot — feels like a real chip under a microscope
Most unique silhouette (Chip): Chip Stack — nobody else has a 3D stacked-die logo
Best hybrid statement: Chip Stack — the three layers literally show "hardware (bottom) running AI (top)"
